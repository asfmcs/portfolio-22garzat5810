Trip to Seychelles, Africa!

By:Emilia Garza T.

Learn all about it's top traditional cousine and the best beaches you can visit...

The Seychelles is an archipelago of 115 islands in the Indian Ocean,
off East Africa. It's home to numerous beaches, coral reefs and
nature reserves, as well as rare animals such as giant Aldabra tortoises.
Mahé, a hub for visiting the other islands, is home to capital
Victoria. It also has the mountain rainforests of Morne
Seychellois National Park and beaches, including Beau Vallon
and Anse Takamaka.

Traditional food you must try!

Seychelle's food is very famous around the world,
and it's part of what makes this place exciting and unique.
From freshly caught fish, to locally grown fruit, from spicy curries to
sweet fried bananas, there is something here for every taste bud and palate.
This experience does not only involve fun activites, but also food
you have never tasted before, which makes your trip more memorable!

Top Food:

Best Beaches

1. Anse Source d'Argent
Anse Source d'Argent is perhaps the best-known beach in the Seychelles,
and certainly one of its most spectacular locations. A real must-see for
any Seychelles holidaymaker and traveler, the powdery sand, crystal-clear water, and imposing
granite boulders make this a uniquely enchanting beach, and undoubtedly worth a visit.

2. Beau Vallon
Beau Vallon is perhaps the most famous beach in the Seychelles,
well-known throughout the country by locals and tourists alike thanks
to its beautiful atmosphere, extensive facilities, stunning sandy surface, and
proximity to some of the Seychelles’ best-loved accommodations.

3. Anse Georgette
Anse Georgette is perhaps the most beautiful beach on Praslin,
and certainly one of the best in the Seychelles thanks
to its incredible mixture of pristine white sand, bright turquoise
ocean surface, and tropical palm trees. A real must-see for
any Praslin holidaymaker!
